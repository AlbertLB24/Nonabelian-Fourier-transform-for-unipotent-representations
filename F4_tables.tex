\subsection{Parahoric restriction tables for \texorpdfstring{$F_4$}{PDFstring}}

Let us clarify some of the notation used in the tables below. The labelling on the representations of $F_4$ used by Reeder is discussed above. The unipotent representations of finite reductive groups of Lie type are labelled as follows. For groups of type $A_n$, these are parametrized by partitions $\lambda$ of $n+1$. For groups of type $B_n/C_n$, the principal series representations are parametrized by double partitions $[\lambda,\mu]$ of $n$. The classification of non-principal series is explained in section \ref{subsec:C_unipreps}. To understand these tables, it is enough to know that groups of type $B/C_3$ have two non-principal series representations $\theta_1$ and $\theta_2$, while groups of type $B/C_4$ have five non-principal series representations $\theta_1,\ldots,\theta_5$. These all come from the unipotent cuspidal representation of their Levi subgroup of type $B/C_2$. 

%but we recall it for convenience. According to the Langlands parametrization, unipotent representations can be labelled by $(x,\phi)$ where $x=su\in G^\vee$ is the Jordan decomposition. Then $Z_{G^\vee}(s)$ is a reductive group of a certain type and $u\in Z_{G^\vee}$ is unipotent. Then we label $(x,\phi)$ by type of unipotent element $u$ in $Z_{G^\vee}(s)$.

For every parahoric subgroup $K_i$, we present two tables. The first one is the table of parahoric restrictions for all Iwahori-spherical representations of $F_4(F)$ of interest. The second table contains the parahoric restrictions of all virtual characters $\Pi(u,s,h)$. The representations of $\overline{K}_i$ are organized in terms of the families. Firstly, we write the characters in trivial families; then we write each family, with the special character always coming first. It is important to remark that not all entries of these tables have been proven; we have not yet explicitly calculated the parahoric restriction of unipotent representations in the block $\Irr(G,K(B_2),\sigma)$, where $K(B_2)$ is the parahoric subgroup whose reductive quotient is $\Sp_4(\FF_q)$ and $\sigma$ is its unique unipotent cuspidal representations. All entries depending on these calculations are conjectured and painted in red.

From these tables, the statement of Theorem \ref{thm:F4} can be easily verified using Lusztig's nonabelian transform. For reference, we write here the virtual characters $\Pi(u,s,h)$ in terms of representations of $F_4(F)$ when $u\in F_4(a_3)$. In the notation below, $\tau=g_2\cdot g_2'$ is another $2$-cycle conjugate to $g_2$ in $A_u=S_4$ but not in $A_{ug_2}=C_2\times C_2$ and, similarly, $\gamma$ is an element conjugate to $g_2'$ in $A_u=S_4$ but not in $A_{ug_2'}=D_8$. The analogous expressions for other unipotent classes are much simpler.
